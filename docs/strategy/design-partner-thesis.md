# Design Partner Thesis

## Why Design Partners Matter

Pluxx is already a credible open-source authoring product. The remaining question is not whether the CLI works; it is which hosted and operational features real buyers will pay for.

That is exactly what design partners should answer.

## The Two Best Early Buyer Types

### MCP Vendors

These are companies with a real MCP that want an official plugin story across multiple tools.

Why they matter:

- plugin distribution is user-facing and strategic
- release quality matters
- platform fragmentation is real
- they feel the pain of maintaining docs and setup across multiple surfaces

### Internal Platform Teams

These are companies standardizing internal agent tooling across teams.

Why they matter:

- they care about policy, approvals, and governance
- they need private catalogs and controlled rollout
- they are closer to the eventual enterprise product shape

## Important Distinction

One design partner should not define the whole roadmap.

Specifically:

- vendor-side design partners validate the official plugin distribution workflow
- internal platform design partners validate registry, policy, and governance needs

Both matter. They should not be collapsed into one customer story.

## Sumble As A Design Partner

Sumble is a strong candidate for the vendor lane.

Why:

- they have a live MCP
- they publicly document setup across multiple AI tools
- MCP access is already part of their commercial product
- they have enough momentum and budget to care about the quality of official distribution

Why they are not enough alone:

- they are not a proxy for internal enterprise governance
- they validate vendor pain, not the full enterprise control-plane thesis

## Design Partner Goal

The goal of a design partner engagement is:

- validate that Pluxx can become the authoring and distribution layer for official MCP vendors
- identify the minimum hosted features worth charging for
- turn product intuition into concrete buyer evidence

## What To Learn

Key questions a design partner should answer:

- where is the real pain today: packaging, setup, release drift, analytics, or governance?
- what would a team actually pay for?
- what hosted surface creates durable value instead of convenience-only value?
- which features are truly required for official plugin support?

## Recommended Structure

Suggested shape:

- `6-8 weeks`
- one product or engineering owner on the partner side
- weekly working session
- direct feedback on setup, packaging, release, and maintenance friction

Potential commercial structures:

- free design partner in exchange for deep feedback and future case-study rights
- discounted pilot
- concierge implementation credited toward a future annual plan

## Internal Guidance

Design partners should be used to validate:

- hosted registry
- release history
- compatibility verification
- publish pipeline
- analytics tied to adoption and operations

Design partners should not be used to justify:

- premature token billing
- built-in model orchestration
- turning Pluxx into an MCP host
