# PLUXX-327 Phase 3 Review

## Verdict

Phase 3 meets its frozen core-four acceptance contract as deterministic `fake-home-install` proof. The 12 CE, Hyperframes, and Superpowers receipts prove generated bundles plus isolated install layout, ownership, verifier behavior, and host registration artifacts. Real-host discovery is environment-unavailable in all 12, activation is unsupported, behavioral runtime is environment-unavailable, and all 324 fixture outcomes retain the registry's declared degradation.

These receipts do not claim installed-runtime or real-host behavior under `docs/proof-freshness.md`. They omit timestamps and commit freshness intentionally; a future installed-runtime receipt must include the host version, host command/outcome, artifact identity, commit, and timestamp required by the canonical proof manifest.

## Review Findings Incorporated

- P1: custom field evidence could bypass global stage truth. Fixed by requiring field/stage status alignment, stage-owned evidence identifiers, exact mechanism matching, and declared-mode retention for unproved outcomes.
- P1: prose-only discovery assertions could look proven. Fixed by classifying all cases as `fake-home-install`, moving Codex/OpenCode registration hashes into installed evidence, and recording real-host discovery as environment-unavailable.
- P1: installed-only facts could promote orchestration behavior. Fixed by requiring behavioral-stage evidence for registry promotion and by keeping the behavior summary false without complete behavioral proof.
- P1: compatibility and doctor truth was hard-coded. Fixed by validating receipt digests and deriving compatibility counts through the compiler-owned receipt summarizer; the installed CLI doctor now gives registry-only guidance without pointing at unshipped test fixtures.
- P1: proven stages could have no exact evidence, and unproved rows could contradict their declared mechanism. Fixed with nonempty stage-evidence schemas and consistent mechanism/status/mode validation.
- P1: compatibility publication could accept self-consistent but stale or misidentified receipts. Fixed by binding the generator to the exact current CE/Hyperframes/Superpowers fixture digests, plugin identities, versions, and 12-case core-four portfolio.
- P1 (final independent review): a self-consistent receipt could still carry stale declared rows after the compiler registry changed. Fixed by validating every declared row against the current compiler-owned registry before resolving or publishing its effective outcome.
- P2: behavioral promotion could survive a failed install stage. Fixed by requiring generated, installed, and behavioral stages to be proven before promotion or a true behavior summary.
- P2: generated receipt identity, inventory, exact facts, and fixture config were under-validated. Fixed with strict schemas, canonical inventory checks, fact-kind checks, host-version consistency, and schema-parsed fixture config.
- P2: product truth still contained Phase 3-pending and all-host orchestration language. Fixed across the roadmap, queue, backlog, decision, phased plan, implementation plan, doctor, and generated compatibility views.

## Residual Risk And Handoff

No executable host adapter consumes `orchestration.generated.json`, so dispatch/context, lifecycle reentry, child-environment enforcement, gates/waits/completion, repair/resume/synthesis/cancellation/fallback, and typed-artifact behavior remain unproved rather than inferred from generated shape. Phase 5 should recover core-four adjunct/distribution topology without broadening the host portfolio; Phase 6 should add the corresponding core-four release gates and canonical installed-runtime/real-host receipts.
